About UUID

UUID (Universally Unique Identifier) is a 128-bit number used to identify information in computer systems. UUID v4 uses random numbers to generate unique identifiers with extremely low probability of collision.

UUID Format

Standard UUID format: xxxxxxxx-xxxx-4xxx-yxxx-xxxxxxxxxxxx

  • 32 hexadecimal digits (0-9, a-f)
  • Displayed in 5 groups separated by hyphens
  • Version 4 (random) indicated by '4' in third group
  • Variant indicated by 'y' (8, 9, a, or b)
